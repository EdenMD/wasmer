Area Topic Sub-topics / Key Concepts
Biology Laboratory Safety and Equipment Understanding and adhering to laboratory rules; Identifying and using common laboratory apparatus safely.
Cell Biology Structure and functions of plant and animal cells; Use of microscopes to observe cells.
Classification of Living Organisms Introduction to the five kingdoms of life; Basic principles of taxonomy.
Human Body Systems Overview of major systems: circulatory, respiratory, digestive, excretory, and reproductive; Functions and basic anatomy of each system.
Nutrition and Digestion Components of a balanced diet; Process of digestion and nutrient absorption.
Ecosystems and Environment Understanding ecosystems, food chains, and food webs; Human impact on the environment and conservation efforts.
Chemistry States of Matter Properties and changes of solids, liquids, and gases; Processes such as melting, evaporation, condensation, and freezing.
Elements, Compounds, and Mixtures Differences between elements, compounds, and mixtures; Methods of separating mixtures (filtration, distillation, etc.).
Acids, Bases, and Indicators Properties of acids and bases; Use of indicators to determine pH levels.
Chemical Reactions Introduction to chemical equations and reaction types; Factors affecting the rate of reactions.
Physics Measurement and Units Understanding SI units and measuring instruments; Concepts of length, mass, time, and temperature.
Forces and Motion Types of forces (gravitational, frictional, etc.); Basic principles of motion and Newton's laws.
Energy Forms of energy (kinetic, potential, thermal, etc.); Energy transformations and conservation.
Heat and Temperature Difference between heat and temperature; Methods of heat transfer: conduction, convection, and radiation.
Light and Sound Properties of light: reflection, refraction, and dispersion; Basics of sound waves and their properties.

Objectives of the Syllabus

  • Apply scientific principles to solve everyday problems.
  • Conduct simple experiments and record observations accurately.
  • Demonstrate understanding of basic scientific concepts and terminology.
  • Appreciate the role of science in society and its applications.
  • Develop skills in critical thinking, analysis, and communication.

Teaching Methodology and Assessment

  • Teaching Approach: Emphasis on learner-centered and inquiry-based learning.
  • Practical Work: Regular hands-on experiments to reinforce theoretical knowledge.
  • Assessment:
    • Continuous assessment through classwork, homework, and practical activities.
    • End-of-term tests and examinations to evaluate understanding.
